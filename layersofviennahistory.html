Layers of Vienna History

St. Stephens is the religious and geographic center of Vienna. Built from the ruins of a parish church consecrated in 1147, the magnificent Romanesque edifice that currently stands was constructed in the fourteenth century and has withstood centuries of conflict. Its multicolored tiled roof stands as an emblem of the city today.

The Österreichische Postsparkasse is a postal savings bank founded in 1882 and opened in January of the following year. In 1906 their current headquarters, designed by the Modernist architect Otto Wagner, was constructed. It is one of the preeminent examples of the Jugendstil, or Vienna Secession style. The use of steel and concrete provide the bank with an appropriate sense of authority and security, which is balanced by the expansive glass skylight that spans its main atrium.
