The Österreichische Postsparkasse

is a postal savings bank founded in 1882 and opened in January of the following year. In 1906 their current headquarters, designed by the Modernist architect Otto Wagner, was constructed. It is one of the preeminent examples of the Jugendstil, or Vienna Secession style. The use of steel and concrete provide the bank with an appropriate sense of authority and security, which is balanced by the expansive glass skylight that spans its main atrium.

der stephansdom

St. Stephens is the religious and geographic center of Vienna. Built from the ruins of a parish church consecrated in 1147, the magnificent Romanesque edifice that currently stands was constructed in the fourteenth century and has withstood centuries of conflict. Its multicolored tiled roof stands as an emblem of the city today.

the secession

Built in 1897, the Secession is an exhibition hall designed by Joseph Maria Olbrich to serve as the architectural manifesto of the Vienna Secession, a group of artists, architects and designers who banded together at the turn of the century to challenge the long-standing academies and artistic institutions of old-world Vienna. Undoubtedly the most famous artist involved in this movement was the painter Gustav Klimt, whose famous Beethoven Frieze is still preserved on the lower level of the hall as an exemplar of the Secession artists’ new radicality.
